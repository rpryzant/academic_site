Paragraph Vectors with doc2vec and gensim

Why I'm writing this

Lots of machine learning algorithms (e.g. logistic regression, PCA, k-means) require the input to be represented with a fixed-length vector. Bodies of text (calling these "paragraphs" from now on) are tricky to cram into a vector. They're of varible length, and they consist of words, which are discrete units.

Fortunately, there's a very clever algorithm that can learn fixed-sized vector representations of paragraphs. It's called doc2vec. Even better, there's a handy, speedy package that implements it for you: gensim! Unfortunately, every single tutorial I could find is broken (except for this somewhat opaque ipython notebook). They don't work with the latest versions of gensim. So I thought I'd write up a little tutorial of my own. Enjoy.
